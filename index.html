About This Project
Project Overview
Prepared for the Onondaga County Legislature to better understand the relationship between housing code violations and the prevalence of vacant or underutilized apartments in Legislative District 15.

🎯 Purpose

Identify patterns in code enforcement, property conditions, and vacancy status to assess how regulatory, economic, or structural factors may be contributing to housing units remaining off the market. The findings support more informed discussions around housing policy, code enforcement practices, and strategies to expand safe, affordable housing within the district.

📋 Key Questions

What is the scope and geographic distribution of housing code violations and vacant apartments in District 15? What violation types are most commonly associated with vacant units, and what barriers prevent them from returning to compliance? How might targeted policy tools or support programs help reduce vacancies while maintaining health and safety standards?

📊 Intended Use

Results may help legislators evaluate whether existing code enforcement mechanisms, incentives, or assistance programs are effectively encouraging compliance — or whether alternative approaches are warranted. Findings could also support targeted interventions such as prioritizing outreach, technical assistance, or rehabilitation resources in areas with high concentrations of violations and vacancies.

🔍 Methodology

Data was sourced from the City of Syracuse Open Data Portal (data.syr.gov) and pulled on February 5, 2026. Analysis was conducted using Power BI with interactive filtering by location, violation type, property status, and ownership to allow stakeholders to explore the data from multiple angles.
